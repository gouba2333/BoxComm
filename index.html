Overview

Why we need BoxComm?

Unlike team sports, combat sports like boxing feature highly dynamic, sub-second actions and demand a significantly higher proportion of tactical commentary (45.6% vs. 21.7% in team sports). To address these unique characteristics, BoxComm introduces a new benchmark based on a three-tier commentary taxonomy—Play-by-Play, Tactical, and Contextual —specifically designed to evaluate both the content ("what to say") and the narration rhythm ("when to say") of automated sports generation.
Key Ingredients

What makes BoxComm different?

🎙️
Category-aware commentary Each sentence is labeled as play-by-play, tactical, or contextual, so models are tested on discourse type rather than generic narration alone.
👊
Fine-grained punch events Detected events carry boxer side, punch technique, target area, and effectiveness to support structured understanding of combat exchanges.
⏱️
Streaming evaluation Benchmark tasks cover both single-moment category-conditioned generation and full-stream commentary rhythm assessment.
