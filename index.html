BoxComm: Benchmarking Category-Aware
Commentary Generation and Narration Rhythm in Boxing

A large-scale dataset and benchmark for fine-grained, category-aware sports commentary generation and temporal narration rhythm analysis in professional boxing.

Abstract

Overview

Bridging the gap between sports video understanding and natural language generation with fine-grained commentary annotation.

Sports commentary generation represents a challenging intersection of video understanding, temporal reasoning, and natural language generation. While prior work has explored automatic commentary for general sports, the unique demands of boxing — including high-speed strikes, nuanced tactical shifts, and dynamic narration rhythm — remain underexplored. We introduce BoxComm, a large-scale dataset and benchmark featuring category-aware commentary annotations for professional boxing bouts. BoxComm provides fine-grained commentary labels across 8 semantic categories (punch types, defensive moves, clinch situations, ring strategy, fighter status, crowd reaction, referee interventions, and round summaries), paired with temporal narration rhythm annotations that capture the timing, pacing, and silence structure of real broadcast commentary. We further introduce BoxBench, a comprehensive evaluation suite to assess multimodal large language models (MLLMs) on category-aware commentary generation and rhythm-aware narration. Experiments on BoxBench reveal significant gaps in current MLLMs for fine-grained boxing commentary, and we propose RhythmFormer, a baseline architecture leveraging rhythm-guided attention and category-conditioned decoding, which achieves substantial improvements over existing approaches. BoxComm and BoxBench together provide a crucial resource for advancing sports intelligence research.

BoxComm Dataset

Illustration of the multi-level annotation hierarchy: video segments → action categories → commentary text → narration rhythm timeline

Figure 1. Overview of the BoxComm dataset. Each boxing clip is annotated with a semantic category label, a ground-truth commentary sentence, and a temporal narration rhythm profile indicating onset timing, duration, and silence intervals.
🎯

Category-Aware Annotation

Each commentary clip is labeled with one of 8 fine-grained semantic categories covering all major events in a boxing bout — from specific punch types to referee interventions and round summaries.

⏱️

Narration Rhythm Labels

We provide frame-level narration rhythm annotations capturing onset time, speech duration, pause intervals, and pitch contour, enabling rhythm-aware commentary generation models.

📺

Broadcast-Quality Video

Collected from professional boxing broadcasts across multiple weight classes, promotions, and commentators, ensuring diversity in style, culture, and technical vocabulary.

🌐

Multi-Language Commentary

Includes English and Spanish broadcast commentary, with automatic alignment between audio commentary and video segments using our proposed AudioAlign pipeline.

🤖

MLLM-Assisted Annotation

Annotation is powered by a novel pipeline combining Multimodal LLM proposals with expert human refinement, achieving high quality at scale with reduced manual effort.

📊

Comprehensive Benchmark

BoxBench provides standardized evaluation splits and metrics for both commentary generation (BLEU, ROUGE, CIDEr, BERTScore) and rhythm prediction (DTW, onset F1, rhythm correlation).

Narration Rhythm

Temporal Narration Rhythm

BoxComm uniquely annotates the temporal structure of broadcast commentary — when commentators speak, pause, and respond to on-screen action — enabling rhythm-aware generation.

🎙️ Narration Onset Timing

We measure the delay between action onset and commentary onset — ranging from near-simultaneous calls (≈0.1s) to reflective post-hoc commentary (≈3s). This creates a rich temporal profile across categories.

⏸️ Pause & Silence Structure

Silence is as important as speech in boxing commentary. We annotate intentional pauses used for dramatic emphasis, audience excitement, and transition between narrative modes.

BoxBench

Benchmark Results

We evaluate state-of-the-art MLLMs and our proposed RhythmFormer baseline on BoxBench, across two tasks: category-aware commentary generation and narration rhythm prediction.

📝 Task 1: Category-Aware Commentary Generation

Models are given a video clip and a category label, and asked to generate a commentary sentence. Evaluated using BLEU-4, ROUGE-L, CIDEr, and BERTScore.

⏱️ Task 2: Narration Rhythm Prediction

Models predict onset timing and speech duration for each clip. Evaluated using Dynamic Time Warping (DTW), onset F1, and Pearson correlation with ground-truth rhythm profiles.
